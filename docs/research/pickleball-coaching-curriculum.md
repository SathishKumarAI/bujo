# Research — pickleball coaching curriculum (beginner → pro)

Curated from USA Pickleball + major coaching outlets (The Dink, Better
Pickleball, DUPR, PrimeTime, Selkirk). Source of truth for the in-app
**Coaching** tab (`src/lib/pickleballAcademy.ts`). Caveats: the 7-day split and
12-week timeline are reasonable syntheses (present as adaptive suggestions);
drill-success % are coaching estimates, not official standards.

## 1. Skill progression (5 levels)

### Beginner — DUPR ~2.0–2.5
1. Rules, scoring, two-bounce rule.
2. Continental ("handshake") grip + athletic ready position, paddle up.
3. Underhand serve (below waist, upward arc, cross-court; get it in, then deep).
4. Deep, high return of serve, then advance to the kitchen.
5. Kitchen (NVZ) rules + line positioning.
6. Intro to the dink — soft, low, into the opponent's kitchen.

### Advanced-Beginner — DUPR ~3.0
1. Shot consistency — reliably land deep serves/returns; placement over power.
2. Consistent dinking — sustain controlled rallies (build to 15–20).
3. Third-shot drop — short swing into the kitchen; net it → swing higher/wider, not harder (#1 skill 3.0→3.5).
4. Footwork — split-step before every shot; two steps in after the drop.
5. Volleys — controlled directional volleys; attack only balls above net height.
6. Basic third-shot drive — paced option so the soft third isn't predictable.

### Intermediate — DUPR ~3.5
1. Purposeful dinking with placement — angle to the backhand, test the feet.
2. Transition-zone footwork — escape no-man's-land with controlled steps.
3. Reset from the transition zone — open paddle, no backswing, soften into kitchen.
4. Drop-vs-drive decision-making — drive pop-ups, drop to control.
5. Speed-ups + intro to hand battles — attack at/above net height when balanced.
6. Intro to stacking — half-stack to keep a forehand central.

### Advanced — DUPR ~4.0 (the leap is mostly cognitive)
1. Consistent resets under pressure — advance only on balls above the net.
2. Offensive cross-court dinking — slice/angle to force pop-ups.
3. Speed-ups and counters — compact punch on shoulder-height balls, then reset.
4. Hand-speed battles — short path, no windup, tip at the opponent.
5. Stacking and poaching — forehands central; poach with a signal.
6. Shot selection / pattern play + specialty shots (Erne, ATP, lob).

### Pro / Expert — DUPR ~4.5+ (cerebral differentiator)
1. Elite hand battles — react + absorb pace with soft blocks.
2. Advanced spin serves — brush/carve to manipulate angle and bounce.
3. Deception — identical mechanics for dinks and speed-ups, sudden acceleration.
4. Advanced Erne / ATP — bait the cross-court dink; move after they commit.
5. Point construction — neutral → offense toward your strength / their weakness.
6. Poaching, switching, adaptive game plans per opponent.

## 2. Practice structure

**Drill far more than you play** (~5 min drilling ≈ 125 reps vs ~15 in a 2-hr
game; pros ~80/20). Recommended **~80/20** for improvers → 60/40 → 50/50 at 4.0+.
Cadence: 2×/week minimum; 3–4 sessions (with recovery) moves 3.5 → 4.0.

### Weekly template
| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Mon | Dinking / soft game |
| Tue | Third-shot drops + transition |
| Wed | Serve / return + footwork (or rest) |
| Thu | Transition zone / resets |
| Fri | Net play / speedups / hands |
| Sat | Live play / games (one tactical intention per game) |
| Sun | Rest or light wall session (200+ touches); ≥1 full rest day/week |

### Single 45–60 min session
~10 min warmup → ~40 min drilling (1–2 techniques) → ~10 min live (80/20).
Solo 30–45 min: 10 wall rallies + 10 serve targets + 10 footwork/dinks.

## 3. Drills by skill (27)
**Dinking:** figure-8 · cross-court rally (50+) · triangle · dink-to-targets · skinny singles · two-ball rally.
**Third-shot drop:** nine-point drill · 0-to-60 · slinky · step-back progression.
**Reset/transition:** catch-the-drive reset · drive-drop-reset · half-volley feeds · yellow-light decision reset.
**Serve:** three-zone depth · target accuracy (10 in a row).
**Return:** deep-return-to-cone · accurate deep returns.
**Volleys/hands:** kitchen sweep · straight-hands battle · dink-then-speedup (firefight).
**Footwork:** split-step reaction · sideways shuffle volley · ladder in/out steps.
**Solo/wall:** wall rallies · wall dink · wall serve/drop targeting.

## 4. Mental game (16)
1. Point-by-point presence. 2. Short memory / flush errors. 3. The patience game (no premature attackulation). 4. Disciplined shot selection (the smart 80% shot). 5. Beating the banger (block & reset). 6. Partner communication (loud + early). 7. Positive partner energy. 8. Pre-point routine (the single most effective tool). 9. Breathing / arousal control (box breathing). 10. Tilt control (re-anchor in the senses). 11. Tournament nerves reframe (nerves = readiness; Yerkes-Dodson). 12. Growth mindset / process over outcome. 13. Confidence is a choice (grounded in training). 14. Body language & self-talk. 15. Visualization / target focus. 16. Controlled aggression (placement over winners).

## 5. 12-week beginner → 4.0 schedule
1 Fundamentals & rules · 2 Serve & return depth · 3 Positioning & movement · 4 Dinking foundations · 5 Dink control & placement · 6 Third-shot-drop intro · 7 Drop under pressure · 8 Transition & resets · 9 Volleys & punch volley · 10 Speedups & hands · 11 Strategy & shot selection · 12 Match play & tournament prep.

**Milestones:** Wk3 reach the kitchen as a team · Wk5 30+ dink rally with intent · Wk7 reliable baseline drop (the 3.5 gate) · Wk8 calm transition reset · Wk10 correct attack-vs-dink decisions · Wk12 complete game + logged matches for a DUPR.
