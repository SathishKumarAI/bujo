# What I'd do with the space (if I were you) + the full backlog

## 1. Space-utilization principles

The redesign goal isn't "fill every pixel" — it's **no dead voids, no needless
scrolling, density that matches the data**. Rules I apply across every view:

1. **No rail voids.** If an aside would be much shorter than `main`, don't use an
   aside — make it a full-width stat bar (what we just did on Monthly). Asides are
   for content that's genuinely as tall as the main column (Today's Wellbeing).
2. **Pair related cards side-by-side** to cut scrolling: Totals + Personal-bests,
   This-week + Coding-minutes, Body-weight + Volume, Location + Goals.
3. **Density where it's data, breathing room where it's input.** Charts/stats go
   tight and multi-column; forms stay single-column and roomy.
4. **Above-the-fold = the daily action.** Today's capture, Gym's session, a
   challenge's check-in must be reachable without scrolling.
5. **Make read-only things interactive.** A calendar day should open that day; a
   PR should chart its progression; a habit name should open its drawer.
6. **Collapse, don't crowd, on mobile.** Side-by-side → stacked under `lg`.

### Applied to Monthly (this section)
Big day trays · clickable days → that day's log · mood tints · full-width
**This-month** stat bar · **Location + Goals side-by-side** · Photo at the bottom.
Next ideas: a habit-completion ribbon under each day, drag an event to another day,
and a "month in review" recap card.

## 3. How to pick next
Default order: finish **passcode encryption** (privacy is foundational) → the
**near-term bounded** wins (fast, visible) → one **medium** surface at a time.
Leave the **large/infra** items until the local-first experience is fully polished.
Each item ships behind a commit + screenshot + a TICKETS row; charts stay lazy and
the bundle stays < 200 KB gzip.
